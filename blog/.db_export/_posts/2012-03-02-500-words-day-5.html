And just like that, I have the opposite problem.
 
This week has felt incredible so far. As far as I can tell, the simple act of writing 500 words in the morning pushed me over the edge from "I should really do [X, Y, Z, ...]" to really rapidly crossing them off my list. I think it's because forcing myself to write makes me enforce my will upon the day. There are no longer allowed to be days where I say "well I'm tired, so I'll just sleep in, go to work, and rest up after. Those days are the "why would I have a blog? I have nothing to say." days. But even on those days, there are still wants/needs just below the surface that I'm not paying attention to, in order conserve energy. I would not have those this was true 5 days ago.
 
So now my problem is: I get excited about producing after my words, that's great. I also get excited about making more progress at night; also great. But I think producing at night, especially at the computer, is cutting into my sleep schedule. I could fill a whole other 500-1000 words, just about sleep, but for now let's just say this: I don't have a problem going to sleep, but I do wake up pretty often, and I literally always wake up feeling tired. I've taken a bunch of steps to get better sleep - some have worked, some haven't worked - long story short, every little bit helps. Two of the big guidelines for me have been 1) Relax before you go to bed, and 2) Eight hours of sleep starting at 2am is not as good as 8 hours starting at midnight.  My nighttime producing is affecting both of those.
 
When I'm excited about nighttime producing, I finish up the day feeling accomplished. This is especially true for now, since my goals are relatively small in scope, under my control, and I can get little wins every day. It's not the "working on hard problems" type of work, where you might spend long periods of time with frustratingly little to show for it. This feeling of accomplishment creates a little cycle of addiction. I mean, I'm almost done with my site redesign, and that's been on my list for years. Now it's just in the tweaking phase, and it feels so good to have that finally accomplished. (Note the stark contrast to the old version of my site.) This gets me excited at night, makes me feel great, makes me want to make more progress. This would all be great if it weren't all of a sudden 1am. The excitement motivates me, but it also gives me more of a hurdle when it comes to relaxing again before bed. It keeps me working later, which starts the clock on sleeping later (And now that my alarm is inflexibly set at 8:30am, it just takes sleep away, period.) Next week shall be the week of balance.
 
So now the problem evolves. How do I get the tail end of my night back under control, but keep the positive feedback loop going? Some options: get back from work earlier; make reading X chapters before bed one of my daily goals; forcibly stop myself, just like I 'forcibly' start myself with 500 words every morning. I'll have to give this some thought.
 
And now, with a brilliant segue, I haven't been tracking my goals. About that...
